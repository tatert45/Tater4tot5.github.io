Branchless Banking App

This project involved creating the branding, research and design for a mobile banking app.

VIEW PROTOTYPE

The Challenge

My vision for the bank and for this app was one that would target audiences that typically feel left out by big commercial banks and financial institutions. The challenge was to create an intuitive, time-saving, banking App that still felet human-centric and personalized for Branchless' diverse population of users. VIEW CREATIVE BRIEF

My Process

Competitive Analysis

I started by researching painpoints users had with major banking apps like Wells Fargo, Chase and Bank of America. the feedback I received from interviews and online forums was: "difficult to find account information and monthly statements," users felt that their was no person-to-person aspect and no easy way to contact customer service through the app. Things I identified what were working well with these apps: the convenience of virtual check-depositing and money transfering/bill pay features.

Branding and Iconography

Because I wanted Branchless to feel different from the stiff, corporate, and impersonal styles of other major banks, I opted for a bright color scheme with a logo that played into the "branchless" foliage theme. I designed several logos and ultimately decide to go with a bright green leaf shape signifying wealth and growth and a modern, rounded sans-serif font that felt clean and crisp.

Sketches & Wireframes

With the painpoints from my user research in mind, I sketched out the main screens for the app, starting with a personalized log-in screen and homepage that featured large and legible CTA buttons. To keep the human-centric feel, I made the option for immediate customer service available on the homescreen "urgent matter" and accross all screens in the bottom nav ("urgent matter"). I then created lo-fi wireframes of all of the different screens I would need for the app so that I could test several user flows and make sure the app would be intuitive to each experience.

User Testing and Takeaways

This app went through many small iterations including the removal of redundant CTA items and busy background images. Users were confused by the "$" icon and noted that the icon to "view/manage account" was different on the homescreen than the bottom navigation, even though both icons lead users to the accounts page. Given more time, I would have created icons unique to this app so all icons would be in sync and offer a more streamlined user experience.
